RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005 - FILING GUIDE

1. WHAT IS RTI?
The Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005, allows any citizen of India to request information from a "public authority" (a body of Government or "instrumentality of State"). It promotes transparency and accountability in the working of every public authority.

2. HOW TO FILE RTI ONLINE
The Central Government and many State Governments have online portals. The central portal is https://rtionline.gov.in.

Steps:
1. Visit https://rtionline.gov.in and click on "Submit Request".
2. Read the guidelines and tick "I have read and understood the above guidelines", then click Submit.
3. Fill in the form:
   - Select the Ministry/Department (e.g., Ministry of Home Affairs).
   - Enter your personal details (Name, Address, Email, Mobile).
   - Select "BPL" status (Yes/No). If Yes, upload proof.
4. Enter the Text for RTI Request application (up to 3000 characters). 
   - Be specific. Ask for data/documents. Do not ask "Why" questions or for opinions.
5. Upload supporting documents if needed (PDF up to 1MB).
6. Pay the fee (₹10) via Internet Banking, Credit/Debit Card, or UPI.
7. Submit. You will get a Registration Number by SMS/Email. Save this for tracking.

3. HOW TO FILE RTI OFFLINE
You can file an RTI application on plain paper.

Steps:
1. Identify the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the concerned department.
2. Write the application on a plain sheet of paper (English, Hindi, or local official language).
3. Subject: "Application under Right to Information Act, 2005".
4. Content:
   - "To, The Public Information Officer, [Name of Department], [Address]".
   - "Sir/Madam, Please provide the following information: [List your specific questions 1, 2, 3...]".
   - "I am affixing the fee of ₹10 via [Mode of Payment]".
   - Your Name, Address, Contact Number, and Signature.
5. Pay the fee of ₹10 via:
   - Cash (get a receipt).
   - Demand Draft / Banker’s Cheque / Indian Postal Order (IPO) payable to the "Accounts Officer" of the public authority.
   - Court Fee Stamp (in some states).
6. Send via Speed Post or Registered Post AD (Acknowledgment Due) so you have proof of delivery.

4. FEES AND EXEMPTIONS
- Application Fee: ₹10 (Standard).
- BPL (Below Poverty Line): Exempt from all fees (must provide BPL certificate).
- Information Cost:
  - ₹2 per page (A4/A3).
  - Actual cost for samples/models.
  - No fee if information is not provided within 30 days.

5. TIME LIMITS
- Standard Reply: 30 days from receipt of application.
- Life & Liberty: 48 hours (if information concerns life or liberty of a person).
- Third Party Information: 40 days.
- Transfer case: 30 days + transfer time.

6. APPEALS
- First Appeal: If no reply or unsatisfied with reply, file "First Appeal" to the First Appellate Authority (FAA) within 30 days of the deadline/response. (No fee for Central Govt appeals).
- Second Appeal: If unsatisfied with FAA decision, file "Second Appeal" to the Central Information Commission (CIC) or State Information Commission (SIC) within 90 days.
